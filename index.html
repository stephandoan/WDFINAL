Distracted?

Why?

We all have distractions in our lives, at least up to varying degrees. Most of us are familiar with the massive drop in attention span that many people have picked up over the past few years. A popular statistic that has been dropped occasionally is that our attention span has dropped to about 8 seconds on average. More specifically, Wyzowl says that we tend to only read around 20% of the words on a web page.

Is that what you're doing here?

Are you glancing at that dog in the corner? It's not leaving. Are you glancing at your phone? For many of us, it's painfully obvious why we're more easily distracted now. For some of us, it started with chain or spam emails with catchy subject lines. Then it was followed by baited headlines and titles of writing that we all shared with each other. Soon, platforms like Vine and Twitter built their entire business models on supporting short, enticing bursts of content. We see Vine's model of concise video has never fallen from grace given Tiktok's success. We absolutely devour shortform content and that makes it infinitely easier for us to be derailed from important matters. Even in politics, it's even easier for someone to bring up a new issue which will eventually overtake something older but still unresolved. The first issue slowly fades away with only a few people trying to draw attention to it.
